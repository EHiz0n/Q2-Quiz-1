Pork Adobo

From the Spanish word adobar and meaning "to marinate", this is where Adobo had received it's name. The marinade of Adobo can come in the form of a liquid or rubbed when using a combination of powdered ingredients. As you can tell, it suggests marinating the pork in both soy sauce and crushed garlic and if you would like something extra you could add vinegar. It is also said that this food can be dated back to the 1600s as the first documented recipe was discovered from a dictionary all the way in 1613 named "Adobo de Los Naturales".

 Pork belly, Garlic, Dried bay leaves, Vinegar, Soy sauce, Peppercorn, Water, Salt.
Beef Nilaga

A popular Filipino dish, Beef Nilaga is made by braising beef in water with other ingredients like potatoes, onions, cabbage, pechay, and other types of greens and produces a flavorful and savory broth. With the beef usually being used are the harder cuts, notably brisket or shank and is cooked for a long duration over open low flame. Because of this, it keeps the beef moist throughout and eventually it tenderizes the meat.

 4 cups water, Salt, 1/2 kilo Beef use beef brisket or beef cubes, 2 medium Onions quartered, 2 medium Potatoes quartered, 1 teaspoon peppercorns, 4 tablespoons Fish sauce, 1 cabbage quartered, 1 bundle pechay leaves, Salt to season
Sinigang

Sinigang is regarded as a classic Filipino soup and known for it's sour and savory medley of flavors. It's usual time of serving is on rainy days to help warm people up and is often paired with steamed rice, making it a popular comfort food for Filipinos. It's comparable to Adobo with how it's associated with a cooking method more than an a particular dish due to it's variations. Protein of many types like pork, fish, shrimp, beef, and chicken and souring items like tamarind, guava, green mango, calamansi, kamias, batuan, santol, and other native fruits can all be used to make Sinigang.

Pork, Tomatoes, Onion, Fish sauce, Gabi, Radish, Vegetables, Tamarind, Banana or finger chili peppers
Chicken Tinola
Alike Sinigang, Chicken Tinola is a popular comfort food. The name can be translated and means chicken stew or soup. It's really similar to that of other chicken soups and eating it is said to be good for the soul. It's steamy, warm, and has an abundance of flavor from the chicken, onions, ginger, garlic, and greens such as malunggay or chile pepper leaves. In present day you may be able to find variations of the food and can add watercress, spinach, taro, long finger peppers, chayote, and more. 		2 lb (907.2 g) chicken wings and drummettes, 5 tbsp fish sauce, ½ yellow onion chopped, 5 cloves garlic minced, 2 inches of ginger julienned, 6 c filtered water more if necessary, 2 small chayote cut into ½ inch cubes, 1 tbsp vegetable oil, 4 c spinach cleaned, 1 c moringa leaves stripped and cleaned, ½ tsp black pepper, salt to taste
Sapos
Literally meaning the word "soup", Sopas is a staple in Filipino cuisine. There are many dishes that can be classified as Sopas, such as pancit lomi and chicken sotanghon, most commonly associated with a soup made of elbow macaroni, vegetables, and milk-infused broth. Flaked chicken is the most popular protein ingredient, but other meats such as ground beef, diced pork, or even corned beef are also used to make the soup.		 2 pounds bone-in chicken parts, 1 onion, peeled and quartered, 1 teaspoon peppercorns, 4 cloves garlic, peeled and crushed, 2 bay leaves, salt and pepper to taste, 10 cups water, 1 tablespoon canola oil, 4 hot dogs, sliced diagonally into ½-inch thick, 1 onion, peeled and chopped, 2 stalks celery, cubed, 2 large carrots, peeled and cubed, 2 cups uncooked elbow macaroni pasta, 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk, salt and pepper to taste, ½ small napa cabbage, chopped, 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved, chopped green onions
